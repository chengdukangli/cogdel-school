Cogdel Launches Four-College System: An Integrated Growth Pathway Connecting Courses, Projects & Practice

June 30, 2026 | Cogdel School Chengdu

Cogdel School Chengdu has officially launched its Four-College System: Muse, Harmony, Quest, and Vigor. These four colleges are not traditional activity groupings — they represent a comprehensive redesign of the student growth pathway for Grades 7 through 12. By integrating professional elective courses, ECA activities, project-based learning (PBL), academic competitions, university preparation, and social practice resources, each college provides students with a directional, coherent pathway to develop their interests into genuine expertise.

How the Colleges Work

In a traditional model, a student's elective courses, club activities, competition training, project outcomes, and real-world practice remain disconnected from one another. The Four-College System reorganizes these fragmented growth elements around each student's core interests. Using Grades 7-12 as a complete cycle, students progress through five stages: interest exploration, direction confirmation, deep accumulation, project output, and achievement showcasing — forming a continuous and coherent personal growth trajectory.

The Four Colleges

Muse — The Incubator for Design & Creativity

Focused on design, art, and new media creativity, Muse brings together students passionate about visual expression and content creation. Its core mission is "to give work a sense of craft, and to build a stage for creative expression." Through graphic design, visual storytelling, digital media production, portfolio development, and exhibitions, students develop demonstrable aesthetic judgment and creative output. Ideal for students drawn to art & design, digital media, filmmaking, and related fields.

Harmony — The Practice Ground for Critical Thinking & Social Connection

Focused on language expression, cultural literacy, and collaborative leadership, Harmony guides students to understand society and engage with the real world. Its core mission is "to learn expression, judgment, and collaboration in authentic social contexts." Through business PBL projects, enterprise practice site visits, social issue research, and public speaking training, students develop the ability to articulate viewpoints, understand others, and drive collaboration in complex situations. Best suited for students interested in humanities, social sciences, business, public affairs, and cross-cultural communication.

Quest — The Laboratory for Science & Inquiry

Focused on scientific inquiry, STEM, and computer science, Quest provides a deep research platform for students curious about "how the world works." Its core mission is "to let evidence reach understanding." Through experimental design, scientific validation, Science Fair research exhibitions, and AI+STEM interdisciplinary learning, students cultivate scientific rigor and research capability. Perfect for students with strong curiosity toward natural sciences, engineering, computer science, and data analysis.

Vigor — The Arena for Academic Thinking & Competition

Focused on academic competitions and analytical rigor, Vigor serves students who want to build competitive advantage through academic depth. Its core mission is "to give thinking a sense of competition and growth." Through systematic competition training, advanced academic thinking development, and logical reasoning, students build confidence and capability in mathematics, physics, chemistry, economics, and other academic challenges. Designed for academically strong students who thrive on intellectual depth and competitive engagement.

From Random Participation to Systematic Planning

In a traditional model, a student who signs up for an elective, joins a club, enters a competition, and completes a project is essentially navigating four disconnected experiences. The college system's value lies in unifying these scattered elements around a core interest direction. A Harmony student, for example, takes Economics as an elective, joins the Business Challenge Club, participates in enterprise visit PBL projects, and focuses university applications on business-related majors — these activities are no longer isolated checkboxes but mutually reinforcing building blocks on a single, coherent trajectory.

Student Growth Pathway

Grades 7-8 (Exploration): Initial exposure to all four directions; broad experimentation to discover genuine interests.
Grades 9-10 (Confirmation): Primary college selection; building directional clarity through professional electives and ECA activities; beginning deep accumulation.
Grades 11-12 (Output): Demonstrating professional depth and comprehensive competence through project outcomes, competition results, academic works, and university application materials.

Colleges as Resource Connection Platforms

The Four Colleges also serve as platforms for connecting external resources. Around each college's professional focus, Cogdel will introduce industry mentors, corporate partnerships, university academic resources, and international practice opportunities. Students will engage with real industries, validate their learning, and produce substantial outcomes — whether design exhibitions, business project presentations, scientific research papers, AI application developments, or sports competition achievements. Under college support, students transform classroom knowledge into tangible, demonstrable results applicable to future university applications and career development.

A Cogdel School Chengdu representative stated: "The college system is not about simply adding courses or activities. It addresses the fundamental question of how students can build continuity around their interests and how what they learn connects with the real world. Moving forward, the Four Colleges will continuously drive the coordinated development of curriculum, ECA, PBL, competitions, and university planning — ensuring every student can find direction, build a portfolio, broaden their horizons, and be precisely prepared for university study and lifelong development."
