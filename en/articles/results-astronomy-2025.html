Cogdel Students Excel in UK Astronomy & Astrophysics Competition

December 16, 2025 | Cogdel School Chengdu

Cogdel School Chengdu students have achieved excellent results in the UK Astronomy and Astrophysics Competition (Junior), applying physics and mathematical reasoning to astronomical scenarios ranging from orbital mechanics to stellar spectroscopy.

About the Competition

Organized by the British Physics Olympiad committee, this competition challenges students to think like astrophysicists. Problems might require calculating the habitable zone of an exoplanet system, analyzing the spectrum of a distant star, or modeling the trajectory of a spacecraft using gravitational assist — all scenarios that demand both physics knowledge and creative application.

Cogdel's Performance

Our students achieved multiple awards at Gold and Silver levels. Beyond the results, several participants reported that the competition sparked a genuine interest in pursuing physics or astronomy at university — exactly the kind of intellectual ignition that competitions at their best can provide.

Student Reflection

"Before this competition, I thought of astronomy as something you look at through a telescope. Now I understand it's a way of thinking about the universe using physics. I'm considering applying for astrophysics at university — something I never would have imagined a year ago."

Congratulations to all participants. May your curiosity about the cosmos continue to grow.
