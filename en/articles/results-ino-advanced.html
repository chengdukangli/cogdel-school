Breaking New Ground! Cogdel Students Excel in INO Advanced Round

February 5, 2026 | Cogdel School Chengdu

Cogdel School Chengdu students have broken new ground by excelling in the advanced round of the International Natural Science Olympiad (INO), a competition that demands creative synthesis across multiple scientific disciplines.

The Advanced Round Challenge

The INO advanced round represents the highest level of natural science competition. Unlike single-subject Olympiads, INO problems require students to integrate concepts from physics, chemistry, biology, and earth science to solve complex, real-world problems. Only top performers from preliminary rounds are eligible to participate.

Why This Achievement Is Significant

Cogdel students' success in this cross-disciplinary competition directly validates our educational approach. The 8-Dimension framework emphasizes connected understanding — the ability to transfer knowledge across contexts and synthesize insights from multiple domains. INO problems mirror this philosophy: they don't come labeled "this is a physics problem" or "this is a biology problem." They come as phenomena to be understood, requiring whatever scientific tools are relevant.

Science Faculty Perspective

"In the real world — whether in research, industry, or policy — scientific challenges don't arrive pre-sorted by academic department. The INO advanced round mirrors that reality. Our students' success proves they're being prepared not just for exams, but for the actual practice of science."

Congratulations to all INO participants. You're showing what science education looks like when disciplines connect rather than compete.
