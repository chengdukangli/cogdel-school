New Semester, New Energy — Every Child Seen and Warm

March 19, 2026 | Cogdel School Chengdu

As the Spring 2026 semester began, Cogdel School Chengdu launched a school-wide psychology and wellbeing program under the theme "Every Child Seen and Warm" — affirming that emotional wellbeing is not a supplement to academic rigor, but its foundation.

Why This Matters

In an academically ambitious environment, a student who feels anxious, invisible, or disconnected cannot learn effectively — no matter how excellent the curriculum. Research consistently shows that emotional safety is a prerequisite for deep learning. At Cogdel, we take this seriously: "Academic excellence and emotional warmth are not opposites. We pursue both with equal commitment," says Principal Lu Kun.

The Program Structure

Designed by Cogdel's in-house counseling team, the program runs throughout the first month of semester and includes:

  • One-on-One Check-ins: Every student meets individually with their form tutor or counselor within the first two weeks — not for academic review, but for a genuine conversation about how they're doing, what they're excited about, and what they're worried about.
  • Classroom Wellness Circles: Structured small-group discussions (8-10 students) in a psychologically safe space. Students share personal goals for the semester and any concerns. The facilitator's role is to listen, not to lecture.
  • Parent Partnership Workshops: Evening sessions helping parents understand adolescent emotional development. Topics include: recognizing anxiety vs. normal stress, effective communication with teenagers, supporting from home without over-managing.
  • Mindfulness & Stress Management: Age-appropriate techniques integrated into the daily schedule — brief morning mindfulness, breathing exercises before assessments, reflection journals.
  • Life Mentor System: Each floor of the boarding residence has a dedicated life mentor who knows every student by name, notices when something seems off, and provides informal, accessible support outside the formal counseling framework.

A Teacher's Observation

"In the first wellness circle, one student barely spoke. By the third session, she was facilitating the discussion herself. Sometimes children don't need solutions — they just need to know someone is truly listening." — Cogdel Form Tutor

What Makes This Different

Many schools have a counselor on staff and call it "pastoral care." Cogdel's approach is different in two ways: First, it's proactive, not reactive — we don't wait for problems to emerge. Second, emotional wellbeing is embedded in the school's operating system, not outsourced to a single department. Every teacher, mentor, and dorm parent is part of the care network.

The program continues throughout the semester with monthly check-ins and an open-door counseling policy. As one student put it: "At Cogdel, someone always notices if you're not okay. That matters more than I can explain."
