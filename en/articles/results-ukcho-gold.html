UKChO Global Gold — Cogdel Student Invited to Cambridge Training Camp

May 9, 2026 | Cogdel School Chengdu

Cogdel School Chengdu is immensely proud to announce that one of our students has achieved Global Gold in the UK Chemistry Olympiad (UKChO), organized by the Royal Society of Chemistry. This outstanding achievement comes with an invitation to the prestigious Cambridge Training Camp — a selective program that feeds into the International Chemistry Olympiad team.

About the UK Chemistry Olympiad

The UKChO is one of the most challenging chemistry competitions worldwide, presenting students with university-level problems that demand creative application of chemical principles to entirely unfamiliar scenarios. It is not a test of memorization — it tests whether a student can think like a chemist. Only the top-performing candidates globally receive Gold awards, and an even smaller subset are invited to the Cambridge camp.

From the Award-Winning Student

"The UKChO problems made me think in completely new ways about chemistry. It wasn't about recalling facts — it was about applying principles to situations I'd never encountered before. That's exactly the kind of thinking Cogdel's approach to science education cultivates."

The Cogdel Science Advantage

This achievement reflects several distinctive features of Cogdel's approach to science education:

  • Lab-First Learning: Our science curriculum maintains a 40% laboratory experiment ratio — students don't just learn about chemistry, they do chemistry.
  • Competition Integration: 100% of Cogdel students participate in academic competitions. UKChO, BPhO, BBO, AMC, and other Olympiads are not extracurricular add-ons — they are woven into the academic program.
  • Subject Specialist Mentorship: Our science faculty includes graduates from Imperial College London, UCL, and other research-intensive institutions who bring genuine scientific passion to their teaching.

What Comes Next

The Cambridge Training Camp provides intensive preparation for the International Chemistry Olympiad — the pinnacle of pre-university chemistry competition. Regardless of the outcome, this student has already demonstrated scientific thinking at the highest level and will carry this experience into university applications and beyond.

Congratulations to our student, the Chemistry faculty, and everyone involved in Cogdel's competition program. This is what happens when talented students meet teachers who believe in them and a system designed to stretch them.
