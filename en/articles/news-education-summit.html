Cogdel Attends China-Foreign Education Innovation Conference

April 9, 2026 | Cogdel School Chengdu

Cogdel School Chengdu was honored to participate in the China-Foreign Basic Education Innovation Conference in April 2026, where leading educators from China and abroad convened to explore the future of international education in an AI-transformed world.

Cogdel's Contribution: Engineering Whole-Person Education

Principal Lu Kun led the Cogdel delegation, delivering a keynote titled "Engineering Whole-Person Education: From Philosophy to Practice." Drawing on her background as a Tsinghua-trained aerospace engineer, she presented Cogdel's 8-Dimension Competency Framework as a systematic, measurable approach to developing what traditional schools call "soft skills" — and what Cogdel calls core competencies.

"Soft skills have been treated as a black box for too long — we vaguely hope children develop them, but we don't track them, measure them, or systematically cultivate them," Principal Lu told the audience. "At Cogdel, we apply engineering logic: define the dimensions, design the sensors, collect the data, iterate the system. Every teacher is a growth sensor. Every semester produces a competency delta."

Principal Lu Kun's Remarks

"We're not importing a foreign educational model — we're building one. One that respects Chinese cultural roots while equipping students with genuinely global competencies. The question isn't whether China can produce internationally competitive talent — we already know it can. The question is: can we do it without crushing the child's spirit in the process? At Cogdel, we answer yes."

Key Summit Takeaways

  • International education is undergoing a paradigm shift — from pure academic outcomes toward holistic student development that AI cannot replicate.
  • Technology-assisted personalized learning is becoming a baseline expectation, not a competitive differentiator. The real differentiator is whether a school can cultivate judgment, empathy, and responsibility — capacities that remain uniquely human.
  • China's international schools are increasingly contributing original educational frameworks to global discourse, moving beyond the import-model era.

Cogdel's participation reinforces its position at the forefront of international education innovation in Western China. The school's approach to measurable, engineering-driven whole-person education generated significant interest from attending institutions.
